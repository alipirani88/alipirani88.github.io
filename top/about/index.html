Hi, I am not sure how to introduce my area of expertise - Bioinformaticist/Bioinformatician/Bioinformagician, Bioinformatics Analyst or Software engineer. I believe my strength lies somewhere between these terms and I am on a constant quest to leverage these skills in developing innovative strategies to improve public health.

I work in Snitkin Lab at University of Michigan and my main focus is to build automatic workflows to process and analyze huge genomics data on shared, Linux-based high performance computing environment.

I don many other hats. To name a few -

  • I do all things bioinformatics,

  • Maintain computational infrastructure,

  • Manage data for various collaborative projects,

  • Interact with faculty, collaborators, post-doctoral fellows and graduate students and implement novel computational methods to analyze high throughput data,

  • Quality control NGS data to investigate possible contamination

  • Optimize processes that helps generate high quality sequencing data,

  • Conduct an annual Microbial Genomics Workshop

  • and the list goes on.

In my previous life, I used to be a Software Engineer with specific interest in web application development. As a software engineer at Capgemini , I was involved in the Product Development through software lifecycle, starting from requirements gathering through successful deployment of web application while maintaining documentation of activities. During my stint, I acquired experience in programming language C# (.NET Framework 2.0 and MS SQL server 2005), MySQL data management and three-tier architecture of software development.

It was this interdisciplinary experience that helped me complete my Master’s in Bioinformatics from Georgia Institute of Technology in 2014.

While I am not working, I enjoy watching movies, cooking, hiking, travelling and learning new skills.
